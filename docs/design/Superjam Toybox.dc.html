Make a jam. Share the jam.

Round 8 folds the last standalone screens into the app: the World gate sits inside the Make flow now (3f) — firing once, as your first jam heads to the workshop — and register-your-builder lives on your profile (5b), where "register one →" already pointed. No more deep-cuts section; every screen has a place in the flow. Sponsor map: ENS names · Dynamic wallets · World ID identity · Unlink + Arc payments · ERC-8004 builders.

3 · Make

Home → idea → follow-ups → plan → builder → the jam

Rethought per Kristjan. The Make tab now has a home: new idea up top, your past jams below — a jam baking in the workshop doesn't block starting another. The follow-up questions are their own beat after you submit the idea (the AI asks, you answer or just type). The plan screen carries the jam's ENS address inline and shows the refine loop as an actual conversation. Before the workshop fires up you choose a builder — registered ERC-8004 agents with on-chain feedback, each earning a cut of what the jam makes. Tapping ⓘ on a builder card (or "who's making this?" while it works) opens its full profile, rendered from fetched agent metadata — description, URL, score, feedback. The World ID gate slots in here too (3f): it fires once, right as your first jam heads to the workshop.

Notes

Round 5 (this revision) applies the team comments: the standalone sponsor sections are dissolved into the flow. Make gets a home with your past jams (one baking doesn't block another), the AI follow-up questions are a separate beat after the prompt, the plan screen is redesigned with the ENS address inline and the refine loop as a visible chat (replacing the ambiguous "updated ✏️" chip), and the reveal ships a copyable deep link — superjam.xyz/kris/tipjarplus. Discover gains the jam page (comments + reviews tabs, World ID human-only) and hosts on-chain mode behind the ⛓ toggle. Inbox is split into Notifications · Friends tabs, with a new chat thread where jams, tips and links travel. Earlier rounds are archived in Superjam Toybox (round 4).dc.html.

Round 6: the builder economy. Anyone can register an AI agent as a builder — it gets an ENS name under yours, an ERC-8004 identity on Base, jam feedback accrues to its on-chain profile, and it keeps a cut of jam revenue. New screens: choose your builder (3d, between plan and workshop) and register your builder (6c). Removed per comments: the on-chain mode screen and the ⛓ feed toggle — the name tag and remix chip on the cards are now tappable straight to Basescan. The pay sheet names Unlink + Arc as the payment rails (wording to confirm). The follow-ups screen lost its confusing second composer — one input, then "Draw up the plan" as the final step.

Round 7: everything gets an address. New Profile section (5a) — name, Dynamic wallet, ✓ human, your jams and registered builders. The maker's badge became a full builder profile (3e) rendered from its ERC-8004 record, opening from the picker or "who's making this?". Pay-a-friend (4d) lives behind 💸 in chat. The follow-ups composer became a "your comments" stack — single lines that send together with the answers. Trimmed per comments: the onboarding footnotes, the ENS pill on the feed card (it lives on the jam page, which now also names the builder), and the World gate moved to before your first jam ships.

Round 8: no more standalone section — the World gate became 3f in the Make flow (fires once, as your first jam heads to the workshop) and register-your-builder became 5b on the profile. The builder picker got ⓘ buttons into each builder's profile, and that profile now leads with fetched agent metadata (description + URL up top, record and feedback below) instead of the ERC-8004 label.

Open questions for next round: does the Friends tab need unread state per friend? Should the jam page's Reviews tab carry a write-review composer distinct from comments? And should "Send to the crew" open the chat picker directly?
